About

I am an operations research practitioner and entrepreneur applying mathematical optimization to complex real-world systems — from Tesla's fleet operations to personalized women's wellness. My work sits at the intersection of constrained programming, data-driven decision-making, and health technology.

I developed the first binary integer programming framework for cycle-based wellness recommendations — accepted for presentation at the 2026 INFORMS Healthcare Conference in Raleigh, NC. I also founded ACorB (Analytics Consulting Organization at Berkeley), now a permanent UC Berkeley program, connecting students to real-world analytics work during one of the most challenging job markets in recent history.

I am pursuing ongoing recognition across Amazon, Tesla, and UC Berkeley, supported by peer recognition through judging and academic publication.
